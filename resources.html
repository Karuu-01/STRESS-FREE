Mental Health Resources

Emergency Help When should you get help ?

The following symptoms might be signs of an existing mental health condition:

  • withdrawal from social activities or relationships
  • inability to cope with daily activities or challenges
  • thoughts of hurting yourself or others
  • frequent or persistent feelings of sadness, anger, fear, worry, or anxiety
  • frequent emotional outbursts or mood swings
  • confusion or unexplained memory loss
  • substance abuse, including alcoholism or use of illegal drugs
  • unexplained physical ailments
  • defiance of authority, truancy, theft, or vandalism
  • dramatic changes in eating or sleeping habits

Get support right away if you're worried about hurting yourself or someone else. Make an appointment with your doctor if you have any of the other symptoms on this page. They can refer you to a mental health professional and other services until they've ruled out a physical cause for your symptoms.

In an emergency, how do you get help?

Are you planning to harm yourself or anyone else? That is a mental health crisis. Immediately go to a hospital emergency room or call the nearest emergency services. For urgent assistance, dial 911.

Type of Providers

Mental disorder is diagnosed and treated by a variety of healthcare professionals. Make an appointment with your primary physician or a nurse practitioner if you think you may have a mental health problem or need mental health help. They will assist you in determining which type of healthcare provider you can see. In certain instances, they may even make a recommendation. For example, they might recommend seeing one or more of the healthcare providers below.
